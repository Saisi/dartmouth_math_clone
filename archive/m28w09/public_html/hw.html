Schedule and Homework Assignments
Math 28, Winter 2009


This is subject to change, though hopefully only minor adjustments.

There are three kinds of assignments made daily:

  • Reading assignments are self-explanatory.
  • Practice problems are not for handing in, but we will discuss them in class. Sometimes this will mean students presenting at the board. These are very important, so while you need not write them up formally please be sure you have thoroughly investigated them.
  • Graded problems will be submitted in class and should be written formally. There will sometimes be overlap between the practice problems and the graded problems, which should be to your advantage. These will be due on the class period following their assignment (except for the first day's assignment, which is due Friday January 9 along with the second day's assignment); if handed in late they will be checked off but not graded. Normally, turned-in homework will be returned at the next class, with some problems graded (you won't know which in advance) and others merely checked off.

The occasional extra credit assignments are exactly that, extra. They are optional, and if more than one is listed, you may do any subset of them. They should be submitted with homework and will be graded using the same standards.

Important Note: As most of you now know, the textbook you acquired (March 20, 2005 edition) is, on account of an error, not the edition intended by the instructor; hence, the numbers of the problems listed below do not correspond correctly. I have fixed them for the problems due on Monday, January 12, and will be fixing the rest as well shortly. In the meantime, if any of you were planning to work ahead, please wait.

Many of these problems are computational, but for those that are not you may want to review my tips on writing proofs.
