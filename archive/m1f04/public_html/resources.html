How do I get Help?

The IAS Program in the First Year Office will be running study groups for this course. Classes will be broken up into small groups for the purpose of reviewing the previous week's materials and for doing homework. An undergraduate tutor will guide each group. Tutorials will meet in the evening on Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday. If you are not currently in a study group, contact your instructor for more information.

Study Tips

You can expect to find a lecture, section or homework problem that you will not understand immediately. How you handle yourself in this situation will determine your success in Math 1.
Remember, it is alright not to understand everything the first time you see it. Calculus is a challenging and subtle subject. The successful student will recognize when he / she does not understand something and will attempt the following steps to acheive understanding.

Just remember the nmeumonic RECESS :

  • Re-read- Reading things over (and over) again can sometimes clarify things you missed the first time around.
  • Experiment- Try playing with examples or related problems and exercises.
  • Classmate- Ask a classmate to explain the problem or try explaining what you don't understand to them. Sometimes formulating your questions can be enlightening.
  • Elective- Do something else for a little while. Take a break from thinking about Calculus and do something you enjoy. After you feel more relaxed, pick up where you left off. A clear mind can work wonders.
  • Study Group- Go to your tutorial and ask your study group for help. The tutors and your peers are there to support you. Use this valuable time to your advantage.
  • See the Instructor- Go to office hours or make an appointment to talk to the instructor. They want you to succeed and will appreciate your input on what part of the material is confusing.
