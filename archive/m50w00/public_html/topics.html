1. Univariate Continuous Random Variables

Distribution and density functions: Quantile, percentile, quartile, median, mode, skewed and symmetric distributions, heavy versus light tails.
Expected values: Mean, variance and standard deviation, central and noncentral moments, moment generating function, expected value of an arbitrary function, delta-method for approximate variance calculation.
Continuous distributions: Uniform, exponential and Gamma distribution, modeling of waiting and life-time, normal and standard normal distribution.
Functions of random variables: Linear transformation, density under arbitrary transformation, linear transformation of normal variable, mean and variance under linear transformation.

2. Multivariate Continuous Random Variables

Major concepts and distributions: Joint and marginal distribution, uniform distribution on plane, independence, conditional distribution and density, bivariate normal distribution.
Expected and conditional expected values: Mean and variance, conditional mean and variance, covariance and correlation, correlation coefficient and its properties, conditional mean and variance for bivariate normal distribution.
Pseudo-random number generation: Independent and identically distributed (iid) random variables, general algorithm for pseudo-random number generation.
Central Limit Theorem and the Law of Large Numbers: Three types of convergence, Slutsky theorem, asymptotic distribution.

3. Principles of Parameter Estimation

Concepts of statistical point estimation: Estimator and estimate, unbiased estimator, linear estimator, efficient estimator, limits to efficiency and Cramer-Rao inequality, asymptotic properties of estimators, consistency and asymptotic normality, small and large samples properties.
Descriptive statistics and exploratory analysis: Estimation of distribution function, histogram, kernel estimation, bandwidth, mean and variance estimation, standard error, estimation of correlation coefficient, median and quantiles, scatter, q-q, and box plots.

 4. Principles of Hypothesis Testing

Major concepts: Null and alternative hypotheses, one and two sided tests, type I and type II errors, Neyman-Pearson lemma, power function and p-value, sample size, critical value, simple comparison test with known variance, Z-test.
Normal based distributions: t-distribution, chi-squared distribution, F-distribution, application to means comparison, exact and approximate confidence intervals.

5. Maximum Likelihood

Asymptotic properties of MLE: Likelihood function, convergence in distribution, asymptotic normality and efficiency, Fisher information, parameter estimation of normal and exponential distributions.
Hypothesis testing in large samples: Wald test and likelihood ratio test, confidence intervals, examples.

6. Major Statistical Tasks

Comparing two samples and ANOVA: Paired and unpaired t-test, testing in large and small sample sizes, one-way ANalysis Of VAriances.
Correlation and regression: Least squares estimation, normal equations, Gauss-Markov theorem, BLUE, assessing the fit, parameter significance and t-statistics, confidence intervals, coefficient of determination, regression prediction.

7. What Can Statistics Do More?

Methods: Nonparametric statistics, robust statistics, analysis of categorical data, multivariate comparisons, nonlinear regression, design of experiments, classification and cluster analysis, time series analysis and forecasting, stochastic process and differential equations, statistical simulations and Monte-Carlo methods, spatial statistics, Bayesian statistics.
Fields: Statistical application in engineering, biology and medicine, economics and econometrics, finance.
