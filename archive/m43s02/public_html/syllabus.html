Syllabus for Math 43
We want to cover as much of chapters 1 through 7 of the text as we can in 9 1/2 weeks.  We'll begin with a study of complex numbers themselves and their basic properties.  This material is covered in sections 1--8 of the text.  Then we'll initiate a study of the calculus of functions of a single complex variable (sections 9--22), and then investigate a few of the so-called elementary analytic functions which we're all familar with from our science courses (sections 23--29).

In the second part of the course, we'll look at the properties of analytic functions which are connected to line integrals.  We'll try to develop enough theory to prove that an analyic function (that is, a differentiable function on an open set of the complex plane) is not only infinitly differentiable, but is actually given by a convergent power series.  To do this, we'll need to develop some notions in topology and analysis.  In particular, we'll need to review and learn more about power series themselves.  This encompasses Chapters 4 and 5 in the book (sections 30-49).  

After completing the second part of the course, we want to develop some applications to evaluating improper (real) integrals.  This will require the residue theory of Chapter 6.  We'll cover as much of Chapters 6 and 7 as we can, with some additional topics if time permits.
