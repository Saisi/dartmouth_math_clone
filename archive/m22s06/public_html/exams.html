FINAL EXAM.  The final exam will given at the time and place scheduled by the Registrar: Friday, June 2nd from 8am to 11am in 102 Bradley Hall.  TAKE NOTE, the exam is not in our usual classroom.  The exam will cover all material covered in class through Wednesday's lecture on May 31st.  There will be no "take-home" portion, and the format will be a blend between the in class and take home portions of the first two exams.  The exam will be closed book.
SECOND EXAM.  The second exam will be given on Thrusday, May 11th during our x-hour.   Just as with the first exam, the exam will be in two parts.  The first will be the in-class portion and will be objective in nature.  I will ask for precise statements of definitions, theorems from lecture or the text and direct applications of what we have studied.  The second will be the take-home portion which will include problems that are more computational or which require more thought or time and which would not be appropriate in a 50 minute time slot.  You are not expected to spend more than two hours on the take-home portion, but no time limit will be imposed.

Your work, and especially your work on the take-home, is expected to be well written and clear.  Unsupported assertions usually will receive little or no credit.

The first exam will cover all material covered in lecture and in the readings up to and including Monday's lecture (on sections 5.1 and 5.2 of the text).  (Recall that we also covered and had homework on part of section 5.4!)  Naturally, the emphasis will be on the material covered since the first exam.  However, it may be necessary to use concepts and techniques from the material covered on the first exam.

The exams have been graded and will be returned in class on Monday.
