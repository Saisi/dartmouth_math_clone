Schedule and Homework Assignments
Math 28, Winter 2008


This is subject to change, though hopefully only minor adjustments.

There are three kinds of assignments made daily:

  • Reading assignments are self-explanatory.
  • Practice problems are not for handing in, but we will discuss them in class. Sometimes this will mean students presenting at the board. These are very important, so while you need not write them up formally please be sure you have thoroughly investigated them.
  • Graded problems will be submitted in class and should be written formally. There will sometimes be overlap between the practice problems and the graded problems, which should be to your advantage. I encourage you to work on the graded problems before the class period following their assignment; this will allow you both to get more benefit from the discussion and to ask any clarification questions as early as possible.

Note that reading assignments and practice problems are always for the following class period unless otherwise noted. Graded problems are assigned daily but collected weekly; in general, assignments made Monday are due Friday of that week and assignments made Wednesday or Friday are due Friday of the following week.

The occasional extra credit assignments are exactly that, extra. They are optional, and if more than one is listed, you may do any subset of them. They should be submitted with homework and will be graded using the same standards.

Many of these problems are computational, but for those that are not you may want to review my tips on writing proofs.
