Math 22: Resources - SUMMER 2006

Theory

  • Online resources for the David Lay book, including transparencies written by a different professor (Lyle Cochran). These can be much more concise than the book.
  • General notes on exam and study technique (geared mainly towards final exams).
  • MIT OpenCourseWare: Linear Algebra has useful practise questions at about our level (but we haven't done 'spaces' yet...).
  • Essay on teaching by Gilbert Strang, author of one of the best linear algebra books. (He also is one of the inventors of wavelets).
  • Beautiful interactive linear transformations applets (by David Austin), showing the effect of T(x)=Ax where A is a 2x2 matrix:
    • Transformation of a single vector (red vector x can be dragged, the result Ax is shown in grey, the matrix A sits at the top of the screen with its entries given by slider-bars i.e. number lines).
    • Transformation of the unit square.
    • Transformation of unit grid lines (showing span of the columns of A).
  • A fixed linear transformation shown with 3d applet. This is the effect of T(x)=Ax where A is 3x2.
  • History of matrices and determinants.
  • Previous incarnation of this course. Another one with some tricky practise final questions
  • Google's Pagerank algorithm explained and again; when d=1 it is simply the eigenvector of the Markov chain given by the web links, whose eigenvalue is 1 ! Also see the original paper.
