INTRODUCTION
    • Combinatorics - study of how / if  (is it possible?) / number of ways we can combine objects into arrangements.

      • Examples:
        1. Combine sports teams into a tournament
        2. Samples of tires into plans to mount them on cars for testing
        3. Students into classes to compare teaching methods
        4. Members of tennis club into pairs
        5. etc.
    • Enumerative Combinatorics - number of ways to achieve an arrangement.
    • Existance of an arrangement
        • Example: Given 10 baseball teams and each team plays each other once, can all games be scheduled if the field is available only for 40 games?
         
    • Combinatoric problems have applications to probability, statistics, physics, biology, CS, chemistry, etc.
