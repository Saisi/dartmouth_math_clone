Business Simulator
is an interactive PC program designed to teach students of business the value strategic decisions making to enhance economic performance

 

Business Simulator (BUS) software consists of four screens/sheets: Income Statement, Cash Flow Statement, Balance Sheet, and Management Decisions Sheet. There are three modes of using BUS: Case Study, Simulation and Game mode. In Case Study mode student can analyze each business decision separately, choosing one of the business performance: goal is achieved (good performance),  goal is partially achieved (satisfactory performance), and goal is not achieved (bad performance). Simulation mode does not give full control over performance so that student can see the result of business decisions as it happens in real life. At last, students (or group of students) can compete playing Business Simulator Game.

Four years of business performance are compared: last, prior, current, and next. Last and prior years are displayd to see previous business performance, as the benchmark of the current and next years performance. Student picks different business decisions and see the consequences comparing the resulted income, cash flow and balance in the current and next year versus last and prior years.
