Optimization and Regression

Eugene Demidenko

Nauka, Moscow 1989

(in Russian)

This book concerns with optimization problems of nonlinear regression. In particular, criteria for existence and uniqueness of the sum of squares of residuals in nonlinear regression are developed. First, criteria for existence of a general continuous function on a non-compact set are suggested. Second, these criteria are applied to sum of squares and illustrated by several popular nonlinear regressions, such as logistic regression, exponential regression, quasilinear regression, CES production function. It is proven that for a nonlinear regression, which cannot be reduced to linear by certain parameter transformation, the probability to have several local minima of sum of squares is positive. Several approaches are developed to verify whether a local minimum of sum of squares is, in fact, the global one, i.e. the solution to the sum of squares is the least-squares estimate. The concept of local unimodality is introduced. A new broad class of optimization functions, as a generalization of sum of squares, is introduced, decompostion functions. This class includes sum of powers of absolute residuals, any convex transformations of the regression space, etc. The theory for the global minimum is generalized to decomposition functions. Also, some general criteria for the global minimum are discussed. Computational methods for minimization of sum of squares and likelihood functions for several statistical models are considered. The developed methodology is applied to heteroscedastic regression models, M-regressions, hyperbolic and quasilinear regressions.
