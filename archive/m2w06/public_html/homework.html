Announcements: 

  • Journals:  We ask that you keep a journal for this course. You need to have a bound notebook for this purpose, clearly labelled with your name.  If you would like, you may use the same notebook that you used for Math 1.
    Unless we ask you to write about something specific, you may write whatever you want in your journal. What you write will be kept confidential. We would like to see things such as questions you are left with after lecture, how you feel you are in your group, any problems you may have with the course and how it is interacting with your life. Anything relating to the course, no matter how obscure, is fair game.These will be graded, but not on their content. We do not expect the journal entries to be long.  We ask that you make at least one journal entry per week.
    Journals will be turned in every Friday, and the instructors will read and comment. They will be returned by the following class period.

  • Written homework problems will be graded as follows:
    Points
    		 Meaning
    0
    		 No work has been done or no cohenent explanation was given.
    1
    		 There appears to be some reasobable attempt at solving the problem.
    2
    		 Either the solution is partly correct, with significant error or omission, or the solution is correct but the explanation is somewhat unclear or incomplete.
    3
    		 The solution is correct, with at most very minor errors or omissions (for example:  a sign error or leaving off a "+C" when evaluating an antiderivative).  The explanation is reasonably clear and complete.
    4
    		 The problem and the solution are explained thoroughly, clearly and completely.  It is easy to read and understand the solution.  The solution is both correct and complete.
