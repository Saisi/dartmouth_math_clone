Supplemental Problems

1.
Prove that if a matrix A has two identical rows (or columns) then its determinant is zero. (Hint: make use of elementary row and column operations)
2.
Suppose that the set of polynomials p0(x), p1(x), ... pn(x) are such that the degree of pk(x) is exactly equal to k. Prove that p0(x), p1(x), ... pn(x) form a linearly independent set of functions (i.e. show their Wronskian is nonzero for a least one value of x).
