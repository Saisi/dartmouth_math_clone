Math 23: Resources - FALL 2007

Theory

  • BOOKS: The only required one is Elementary Differential Equations and Boundary Value Problems (8th edition) by Boyce and DiPrima (Wiley, 2004), available at Wheelock Books in town. There is a Student companion site with reviews, quizzes, Matlab and other files, by chapter.
    Some other optional interesting-looking (but untried by myself) books include Selwyn Hollis and John Polking et al.
  • Previous versions of this course (scroll down to Math 23).
  • MIT's excellent OpenCourseWare Diff Eq 18.03 course including complete lectures, videos, worksheets.
  • Steve Zelditch's ODE course uses our book, and has his own lecture notes, practise exams, and HW solutions.
  • My 1-page intro to 2-by-2 matrices.
  • Rob Hladky's crash courses on basic ODE terminology and partial derivatives.
  • Craig Tracy's elegant course notes on ODEs.
  • Russian solution techniques compendium EqWorld (go to ODEs, PDEs, etc).
  • Some Quicktime movies of ODE systems and applications.
  • John White's Diff Eq course notes.

Tools

  • Java applet versions of dfield and pplane that let you numerically explore 1-variable and 2-variable ODEs in your browser.
  • Printing out stuff at Greenprint stations and info on installing Greenprint to your computer.
  • Online graphing applets (quick and dirty): fooplot. Matlab code to plot a simple graph: graph.m

Other / Fun

  • Soda Constructor allows you to explore normal modes in arbitrary 2D mass-spring systems (turn off gravity and friction for that). This is a coupled system of second-order linear ODEs, which could be reduced to a coupled system of first-order ODEs as in 7.6 (example 3) in the book. It's also a lot of fun to play with and has its own online community!
  • Ocean waves are described by PDEs too.
  • Much of my research actually involves solving a PDE eigenvalue problem arising from Laplace's equation, known as the Dirichlet eigenproblem.
