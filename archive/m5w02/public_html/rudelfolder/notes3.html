Notes From Monday 2/18

 

Definition: (mentioned last Wednesday): A subgroup  is a subset W of a group G with the following characteristics:

        i) if X is in W, so is its inverse.

        ii) If X and Y are in W, then so is X*Y.

 

Definition: The image of a group under a map f  is everything that "gets hit" by f.  That is, if f  takes G to H, then the image of f  is any element in H that is equal to f(g) for some element g in G.

 

Theorem:  If f  maps G to H then the image of f  is a subgroup of H.

 

Maps on groups:  A map from one group to another can be constructed in precisely the same way a map is constructed on a free group, but one must be careful that any relations present in the first group are respected by the map.

 

Example:  if G is the group attained by starting with the free group on {a} and imposing the relation {aaaa=e}, and H is the free group on {b}, then one cannot construct a map that takes [a] to [b], as then [aaaa] would have to go to [bbbb].  But [aaaa]=[e] so [aaaa] must go to [e], and [bbbb] does not equal [b] in H.
