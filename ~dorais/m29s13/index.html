Math 29 — Spring 2013

Introduction to Computability

Ask not what you can do for your computer
—
Ask what your computer can do for you!

  • What are the barriers and limitations of computers?
  • What is computable and what isn't?
  • What do we know about the uncomputable?
  • What is predictable and what is random?
  • Can we ever certify that a program is bug-free?
  • Can we measure the complexity of a problem?

These are a few of the questions we will tackle in Math 29 this Spring!

There are no formal prerequisites for Math 29. We will cover background material as needed and when relevant, and as long as you're willing to learn to work abstractly and to read and write proofs, you are sufficiently prepared!
