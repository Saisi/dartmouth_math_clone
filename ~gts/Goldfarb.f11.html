Homotopy methods in topological rigidity

Boris Goldfarb

SUNY Albany


One of the main open problems in topology is to establish a
kind of rigidity similar to that in the recently verified Poincare
conjecture. Often two manifolds that are homotopy equivalent are in
fact homeomorphic. This is regularly observed in geometry for two
closed manifolds with contractible universal covers. In fact,
topological rigidity is believed to hold for all such manifolds, also
known as closed aspherical manifolds. I will describe one modern
approach to proving this old conjecture of Armand Borel and the recent
results obtained with a number of collaborators.
