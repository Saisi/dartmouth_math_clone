Sperner, Tucker and Borsuk-Ulam type theorems on manifolds

Oleg Musin

University of Texas Brownsville


The famous Sperner and Tucker's lemmas are combinatorial analogs of the famous Brouwer and Borsuk-Ulam theorems. These lemmas have been used for effective computation of fixed points, in root-finding algorithms, and are applied in fair division in algorithms. In this talk will be considered several generalizations of these lemmas and present constructive (algorithmic) proofs. Also will be discussed a Borsuk-Ulam type theorem for PL-manifolds with a finite group action, depending on the free equivariant cobordism class of a manifold. In particular, necessary and sufficient conditions are considered for a manifold with a free involution to be a Borsuk-Ulam type.
