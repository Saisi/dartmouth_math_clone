Isospectral hyperbolic 2-orbifolds

Peter Doyle

Dartmouth College


Isospectral hyperbolic 2-orbifolds have the same number of
cone-points of each order, provided that you count a corner of angle
pi/n on the reflecting boundary as half a cone-point of order n. The
corresponding result does not hold for Euclidean 2-orbifolds, where
there are counter-examples, though you might not like them, because
they are disconnected.
