Combinatorics of knots: 4-valent graphs and graph links

Denis Ilyutko

Moscow State University

Classical knot diagrams can be encoded by Gauss diagrams, but not each chord diagram is a Gauss diagram of a classical knot diagram. Considering all chord diagrams one gets the theory of virtual knots, introduced by Kauffman. To a Gauss diagram one corresponds its intersection graph. This is a simple graph whose vertices are in one-one correspondence with chords of the Gauss diagram, and two vertices are connected by an edge whenever two chords are intersecting. As a result we obtain the theory of graph-knots (Ilyutko-Manturov) or looped interlacement graphs (Traldi-Zulli). We discuss invariants for these objects and equivalence of different approaches to their study.
