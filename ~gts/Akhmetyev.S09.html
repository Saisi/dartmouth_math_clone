A geometric solution of the Kervaire Invariant One problem

Pyotr Akhmetyev

IZMIRAN, Russia


It is well-known that a framed closed manifold $(M^m,\Psi)$ of dimension
$4k+2$ determines an algebraic invariant $\theta((M^m,\Psi) \in \Z/2$
called the Arf-Kervaire invariant. This invariant characterizes exotic
smooth structures on spheres $S^{4k+1}.$

The Kervaire problem is to find all integers $m=4k+2$ such that there
exists a closed framed manifold $(M^m, \Psi)$ with the nontrivial
Arf-Kervaire invariant.
We proved that there exists an integer $m_0$ such that every framed
manifold of dimension $m=4k+2\ge m_0$ has the trivial Arf-Kervaire
Invariant.

In my talk I will present a sketch of the proof of this Theorem.
