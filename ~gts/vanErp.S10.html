The use of noncommutative topology in index theory

Erik van Erp

Darmouth College


In the early 1960s Atiyah and Singer derived a cohomological
formula that computes the Fredholm index of an elliptic operator. This
single formula generalizes such classical results as the Gauss-Bonnet
theorem, the Riemann-Roch theorem, and the Hirzebruch signature
theorem.

In subsequent decades, especially in the 1980s, work on the analytic
K-theory of noncommutative C*-algebras greatly clarified the proof of
the index formula, leading to many further generalizations. One
difficulty with some of these generalizations is that it is very hard
in practice to compute, for an analytically defined K-cycle, the
corresponding topological K-cycle.

As an example of this situation I will discuss the solution of the
index problem for certain hypoelliptic operators on contact manifolds.
The final formula can be stated in purely topological terms, as a
modification of the Atiyah-Singer formula, but all the proofs involve
heavy use of noncommutative topology. I will not attempt to sketch
these proofs, but hope to outline some of the framework in which the
problem can be understood.
