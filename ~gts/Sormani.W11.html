Applications of intrinsic flat convergence

Christina Sormani

CUNY


The intrinsic flat distance between oriented compact Riemannian
manifolds with boundary was introduced in work with S. Wenger (UIC)
applying methods of Ambrosio-Kirchheim. After reviewing this
new concept, we will discuss potential applications to the stability of
Euclidean space in the Positive Mass Theorem of Schoen-Yau and
the stability of Schwarzschild space in the Penrose Inequality.
Preliminary work with D. Lee (CUNY) covering the rotationally symmetric
case leading to appropriate conjectures in the general case will
be presented. The presentation is constructive and Riemannian
and does not require knowledge of Geometric Measure Theory. Doctoral
students are welcome to attend.
