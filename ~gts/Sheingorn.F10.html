What I do (or anyway, have done)

Mark Sheingorn

CUNY


I study curves of several types on surfaces that are quotients of the
upper half plane by (most often a low index) subgroup of the modular group. This
is admittedly a very limited setting, but it is one where number theory affords
explicit computation and sometimes characterization of trajectories. Groups
arising include $\Gamma (1), \Gamma ', \Gamma (3), \Gamma^3$ and $\Gamma(p) \cap
\Gamma^0(p^2)$, where $p$ is prime and $p \equiv 1(4).$

Almost always the number theory drives the geometry ... that is, we geometers
(you geometers?) don't know enough about the trajectories to deduce number
theoretic information beyond what is known.

Related number theory includes the Ankeney-Artin-Chowla conjecture, the Hardy-
Littlewood circle method and the Markoff equation $x^2 + y^2 +z^2 = 3xyz$.

Curves arising are simple geodesics closed and other-wise and horocycles.

This talk will be an introduction. If there is interest, I can actually prove
things on my return from NYC after tax day.
