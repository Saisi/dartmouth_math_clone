Isospectral orbifolds with different maximal isotropy orders

Dorothee Shueth

Humboldt University


We construct pairs of compact Riemannian orbifolds which are isospectral
for the Laplace operator on functions such that the maximal isotropy order
of singular points in one of the orbifolds is higher than in the other.
In one type of examples, isospectrality arises from a version of the
famous Sunada theorem which also implies isospectrality on p-forms; here
the orbifolds are quotients of certain compact normal homogeneous spaces.
In another type of examples, the orbifolds are quotients of three-dimensional Euclidean space and are shown to be isospectral on functions using dimension formulas for the eigenspaces developed by Miatello and Rossetti. In the latter type of examples the orbifolds are not isospectral on 1-forms.


This is joint work with Juan Pablo Rossetti and Martin Weilandt.
