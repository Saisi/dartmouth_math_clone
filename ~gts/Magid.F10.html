Timelike Minimal Surfaces and the Timelike Bj\"orling Problem via the Split Complex Numbers

Martin Magid

Wellesley


The split-complex numbers $\mathbb C' =\{t + ks | t, s \in \mathbb R, k^2=1, 1k=k1\}$ is a commutative algebra over $\mathbb R$. I will define split-holomorphic functions $f: \mathbb C'\to \mathbb C'$, and give a representation theorem for timelike minimal surfaces in Lorentz three-space using split-holomorphic one-forms.

Then I will apply this representation theorem to find two new Weierstrass formulas, which allow for easy, local, construction of timelike minimal surfaces, and to the solution of the timelike Bj\"orling problem.
