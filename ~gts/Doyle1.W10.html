How to look at a Markov Chain

Peter Doyle

Dartmouth College


I will argue that the theory of ergodic Markov chains should be
approached through a consideration of bilinear forms on the hyperplane
{x: \sum_i x_i=0}. This leads to a simple understanding of
fundamental results, like the theorem of Kemeny-Snell that a chain is
determined by its matrix of hitting times. And it indicates how to
portray the chain in Euclidean space.
