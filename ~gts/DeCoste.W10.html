Heisenberg-like Lie groups

Rachelle DeCoste

Wheaton College (Massachusetts)


Two-step nilpotent metric Lie algebras of Heisenberg type have been studied extensively. In their 2000 paper, Gornet and Mast define Heisenberg-like Lie algebras as a natural generalization of those of Heisenberg type. In this talk I will discuss geometric properties, alternate characterizations and infinite families of examples of Heisenberg-like Lie algebras. The work discussed is joint with Lisa DeMeyer of Central Michigan University and Maura Mast of University of Massachusetts, Boston.
