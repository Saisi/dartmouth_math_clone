Moduli space of CP^1 structures and CMC surfaces in R^3

Rob Kusner

UMass, Amherst


Complete embedded constant mean curvatures (CMC) surfaces in \R^3 seem
to be highly transcendental objects, and their moduli spaces are
generally only understood in a few special cases. In this talk, we
will reveal a surprising connection to \CP^1-structures and
holomorphic solutions to Hill's equation U_{zz} + q(z)U = 0, where
q(z) is the Schwarzian of the developing map for the \CP^1-structure.
This allows us to explicitly work out the moduli space of k-ended
coplanar CMC surfaces of genus 0. We'll also discuss some related
potential applications, including an explicit description of minimal
surfaces in S^3.
