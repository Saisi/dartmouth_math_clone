Teichmueller's Extremal Maps and Quasiconformal Homogeneity

Petra Bonfert-Taylor

Wesleyan University


A hyperbolic surface $S$ is strongly $K$-quasiconformally homogeneous if for all $x,y\in S$ there exists a $K$-quasiconformal homeomorphism $f:S\to S$ which is homotopic to a conformal automorphism of $S$ and maps $x$ to $y$. After defining/reviewing all necessary concepts we will show that there exists a constant $K_c>1$ such that necessarily $K > K_c$, unless $S=\mathbb{H}^2$. We will show how the sharp constant $K_c$ is computed in terms of the diameter of the (2,3,7)-hyperbolic orbifold and Teichm\"uller's extremal map and finally prove that $K_c$ cannot be attained for any strongly quasiconformally homogeneous surface.
