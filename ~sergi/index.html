I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Mathematics at Dartmouth College.

My research interests are mostly in enumerative and algebraic combinatorics. My work so far involves permutations, patterns, bijections, triangulations, lattice paths, generating functions, algorithms, number theory, and applications of combinatorics to computational biology and dynamical systems.

Here you can download my CV and publication list with abstracts. After finishing my Ph.D. at MIT with Richard Stanley in 2004, I have been a Postdoctoral Fellow at MSRI, and a John Wesley Young instructor at Dartmouth.

I co-organized the conference Permutation Patterns 2010. The proceedings have been published in a special issue of Pure Mathematics and Applications.
