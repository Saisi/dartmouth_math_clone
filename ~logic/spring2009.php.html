Jun 1
Tom Kern
Dartmouth College
 Russell's Socks and Equivalent Failures of the Axiom of Choice (Honors thesis presentation)

Bertrand has a problem with his countable set of pairs of socks! He wants to find an infinite set of socks such that no two socks are from the same pair, but he isn't able to without the Axiom of Choice! I will construct a model of set theory in which this unusual collection exists and discuss other oddities whose existence is logically equivalent to the existence of Russell's Socks.

All are invited. I will attempt to make this material as accessible as possible, which will most likely put it at an upperclass undergraduate level.
