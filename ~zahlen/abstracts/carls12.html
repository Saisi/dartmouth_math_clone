For an integer n> 2, the unit group modulo n has an even number of elements, with half of them having representatives in (0,n/2) and the other half having representatives in (n/2,n). It is "balanced". Say a subgroup H of this unit group is "balanced" if each coset of H is evenly split between the bottom half and the top half. Suppose g>1 is a fixed integer. We are concerned with the distribution of numbers n coprime to g for which the cyclic subgroup in the unit group mod n is balanced. This has an application to the statistical study of the rank of the Legendre curve over function fields. (Joint work with Douglas Ulmer.)
