Abstract: Humankind (more accurately, a tiny subset thereof) has long been concerned with unlocking the mysteries of the primes. In the 19th century, substantial progress was made on such questions by analytic methods, leading to proofs of Dirichlet's theorem on primes in arithmetic progressions and the celebrated prime number theorem. And as we heard in Dan Goldston's Kemeny lectures last week, prime number theory continues to be an active and fruitful area of modern research. Nevertheless, many of the most natural and innocent-sounding questions (such as the twin prime conjecture) remain unanswered. I will discuss what is known about some of these problems if we replace the ring of integers by the ring of polynomials over a finite field.
