Abstract:


The Markoff value {\em MV} of a real number $\alp$ is defined as a certain limsup over a set of reals derived from the continued fraction of $\alp$. It has been known since the '80's that the set of $\alp$ with $MV(\alp) = 3$ parametrize  the infinite simple geodesics on \threesurf, \,  \gammaprsurf, and   \sphsurf. In a previous paper \cite{mS} we showed  that the Hausdorff dimension  of the subset of $\{\alp \: | \: MV(\alp) = 3\}$ where the limsup  is achieved has Hausdorff dimension  zero.


Here  we show that there is a subset of $\{\alp \: | \: MV(\alp) = 3\}$ where the limsup is NOT achieved that is a Cantor set of positive Hausdorff dimension. Indeed we can show this dimension is greater than  $0.2675967907\ldots$
