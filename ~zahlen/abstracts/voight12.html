An expander graph is one with small diameter: one can get from any vertex to another with a short path, a desirable property for the purposes of network communications, for example. The first explicit constructions of such graphs were given by Ihara, Lubotzky-Phillips-Sarnak, and Margulis. In this talk, we describe a generalized construction arising from Hilbert modular forms, and an application to computing ideal classes in definite quaternion orders. This is joint work with Markus Kirschmer.
