The problem of finding the primes p for which an integer a is a primitive root modulo p has been of interest to mathematicians dating back to Gauss. In 1927, Artin famously conjectured that, if a is not a square and not -1, there are infinitely many primes with a as a primitive root. We will discuss several approaches to this problem, including the celebrated proof (conditional upon the Generalized Riemann Hypothesis) due to Hooley.
