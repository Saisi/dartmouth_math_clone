Abstract: In this expository talk, we will examine Davenport's papers on asymptotics for the number of irreducible binary cubic forms of discriminant up to X, as X tends to infinity. This is achieved by reducing the original problem to a lattice-point counting problem, and then applying a "principle of Lipschitz". Applications of this work include asymptotics for counting binary cubic number fields, of Davenport and Heilbronn, and its generalizations by Manjul Bhargava to counting $S_{4}$ quartic fields and $S_{5}$ quintic fields.
