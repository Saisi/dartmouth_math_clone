Abstract: Following terminology from antiquity, a natural number is said to be abundant if it is smaller than the sum of its proper divisors. Since Davenport, we know the abundant numbers have a positive asymptotic density, and from Behrend we know that this density is between $0.24$ and $0.32$. Henri Cohen asked if it could be determined whether it is less than, equal to, or more than $1/4$. This was settled by Del\'eglise when he computed that it is $0.247\dots$. We will discuss recent improvements to the algorithm of Del\'eglise which allows us to discover the next decimal digit.
