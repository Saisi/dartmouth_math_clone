Abstract:

A congruent number is an integer which is the area of a right triangle whose sides all have rational lengths. So 6 is a congruent number as the area of a 3-4-5 right triangle. But 5 is also a congruent number as is 157. What do these have to do with Pythagorean triples and rational points on curves? Fermat's Last theorem even makes a brief appearance as part of the discussion.

This is a warm up for an undergraduate talk I am to give on this topic on Monday, so it will be quite elementary, but still a pretty confluence of ideas. At the end, we may address deeper connections.
