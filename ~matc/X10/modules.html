X10 Course Instructional Modules

This page provides Web based access to interactive graphics programs for teaching statistical concepts in introductory statistics courses. They are designed to be as intuitive as possible, with no mathematical notation. They may be demonstrated in classrooms or used in homework assignments. Many other "applets" of this sort are available at the links we provide for teaching resources.
  • Drawing Statistical Conclusions About Proportions

  • Drawing Statistical Conclusions About Proportions Bayesian Version

    These applets help demonstrate the concept of a "p-value". The goal is to help students avoid some of the common misconceptions and misuses of this important statistic.

  • Slides for p-value applet

    These html pages were designed to be used with a presentation of the p-value applets in a classroom (hence the big font).

  • Comparing More Than Two Means: Analysis of Variance
    (Use Internet Explorer 4 to view this on a Macintosh)

    This applet illustrates graphically the key features of analysis of variance as it might be taught in an introductory course.
