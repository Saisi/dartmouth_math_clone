The WNA revisited--cavity shape effects

In Section 3.2 we have assumed that generic fluctuating quantities such as and and have a white noise power spectrum for . In section 4.1 we are going to suggest that this white noise assumption is approximately true for any fluctuating quantity that comes from a normal deformation (the term `normal' will be defined there).

Obviously, the goodness of the `white noise assumption' in the two cases mentioned is related to the chaoticity of the system, and should be tested for particular examples. This has been done so for the cavity of Fig. 3.2 (see [14], and Figs. 3.4 and 4.2). This cavity is an example of a `scattering billiard' and so exhibits strong chaos (no marginally-stable orbits) [40]. If the motion is not strongly chaotic we may get a that decays like a power law (say with ) rather than an exponential [40,78,54,37] (time of crossover to algebraic decay is discussed in [53]).
