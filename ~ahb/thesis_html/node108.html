Trap properties

In the bulk of this Section we will examine the atomic waveguide properties for light nearly resonant with the D2 line of cesium, using an optical guide of index 1.56 of the dimensions and from Section 8.2.2, and a substrate of unity index. The saturation intensity for cesium is 11.2W/m [2], and its resonant wavelength of 852nm requires that the physical guide size is 0.83m by 0.21m. (At the end of the Section we present preliminary calculations for a substrate and a different guide, and discuss how the atom waveguide properties are changed).

Given the guide, we are free to choose three experimental parameters, namely the optical powers carried in the two modes, and the detuning (assumed to be symmetric, that is, to be of equal magnitude for red and blue beams, because little advantage can be gained with an unsymmetric detuning).
