The scaling method in action

In Figs. 6.5 and 6.6 I show that the (and tensions ) found coincide with tension minima found by the sweep method of the previous chapter. The agreement is well within the sweep method error due to the finite rounding of the minima. Because the scaling method does not just compute the tension of a single state, it is able to distinguish close eigenvalues that cannot be distinguished using the sweep method (using the same basis set). How far this can be pushed has not yet been investigated. The finite tension minima are highly correlated between the methods, and are therefore a function of the limitation of the RPW basis set chosen.
