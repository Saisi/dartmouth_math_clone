This issue is identical to the divergence of basis coefficients found by Dietz et al. when attempting to expand a `cake billiard' eigenfunction in terms of plane waves (or angular-momentum states) [63]. The problem is that of representing evanescent components, which are generally present in billiard eigenfunctions, by distributions of real plane waves. As Berry has shown [26], rapidly-oscillating distributions are required which grow exponentially with the number of wavelengths across the system and with the evanescence parameter.
