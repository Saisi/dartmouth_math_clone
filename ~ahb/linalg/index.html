  • Beautiful interactive linear transformations applets (by David Austin), showing the effect of T(x)=Ax where A is a 2x2 matrix:
    • Transformation of a single vector (red vector x can be dragged, the result Ax is shown in grey, the matrix A sits at the top of the screen with its entries given by slider-bars i.e. number lines).
    • Transformation of the unit square.
    • Transformation of unit grid lines (showing span of the columns of A).
  • A fixed linear transformation shown with 3d applet. This is the effect of T(x)=Ax where A is 3x2, as discussed in Lecture of 9/30/03.
  • Vector calculator applet can find linear dependence relations for an arbitrary number of vectors. It can display in 2d and 3d too! Matrix multiplier applet can multiply matrices and matrix calculator applet can invert them and do other operations. You can use to check (but not do) your HW!
  • Brief history of linear algebra (minus the Chinese in 250 BC).
