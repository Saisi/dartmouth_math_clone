Research

My research interests lie in ares of both pure and applied mathematics. In "pure" directions, I have studied problems in Carnot-Caratheodory geometry such as minimal and isoperimetric surface problems in Carnot groups. In the past few years, I have focused primarily on problems in applied mathematics including

  • Applications of sub-Riemannian geometric models of the visual cortex.
  • Network models in the study of complex systems with applications to economics, genetics, psychology, and neuroscience.
  • Spectral methods in network analysis
  • Data analysis in general
 

 

Here is a list of papers and preprints.  Many can be found on arxiv.org, but the versions below (if available) are likely up to date and (perhaps) have better quality pictures.
