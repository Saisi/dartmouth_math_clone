Readings

The readings (see pre-course work) will be:

  • James Zull, "The Art of Changing the Brain" (2002).
  • Richard R. Skemp, "The Psychology of Learning Mathematics," expanded American edition (1987)
  • Eleanor Duckworth, "The Having of Wonderful Ideas and Other Essays on Teaching and Learning," 2nd edition (1996)
For Duckworth you'll want to read a bit on Piaget before you start, eg here. Watching the videos of kids is fun too (lots more on youtube).

Required books to buy

  • Steven Krantz, "How to Teach Mathematics," 2nd edition (AMS, 1999).
  • John Meier and Thomas Rishel, "Writing in the Teaching and Learning of Mathematics" (MAA, 1998).
  • E. Rogers, N. Davidson, and B. Reynolds, "Cooperative Learning in Undergraduate Mathematics: Issues and Strategies That Work" (MAA, 2001). Out of print and available as photocopy in binder - ask at the math office

Timeline and Assignments

The summer teaching course will be June 27 - August 16, with no classes on July 4 or July 5. Please block out 1-4pm each day in the regular course; some weeks we'll only use 4 of the 5 days. High school math camp will be the weeks of July 15 through 19 and July 29 through August 2 (please block out noon-5pm all days these weeks).

The final week, August 12 through 16, will probably be devoted to guest teaching and not include any class meetings, although this isn't guaranteed; we may need to move some classes to work around the guest teaching schedules of graduate students, or the schedules of our guest speakers.

Resources and Articles

  • Classroom Assessment Techniques (CATs), and also here, for rapid formative evaluation. For formative and summative assessment specifically of collaborative learning, see this article by Walker and Angelo (1997).
  • Alex's advice on how to write good worksheets and use them in class
  • Clarity in lesson plans: Hativa, N. (1983). What makes mathematics lessons easy to follow, understand, and remember. The College Mathematics Journal, 14, 398-406. Statistically bogus but still a great summary of good techniques.
  • Rouviere on coop learning ideas in math teaching, Brown on guidelines for group work in math. (Both read on day 7).
  • Jenny's notes on classroom management.
  • Bloom's taxonomy of cognitive skills: Taxonomy in math, Shorser on undergraduate mathematics.
  • Pygmalion Effect (day 11): encyclopedia review, useful summary, another summary with useful lists, recent status.
  • Learning styles (day 11): Felder and Solomon, Felder and Silverman 1988. Meet your students: Sensing/Intuitive, Sequential/Global, Extrovert/Introvert. Recent commentary in the Chronicle
  • Dave Raggett's intro to HTML. Make this a file called index.html in directory public_html in your gauss home directory.
  • Presentation from Cindy Tobery at DCAL: handout, and slides.
  • Racial microaggressions article.
  • Why So Few? report on women in science and math (AAUW, 2010). PNAS article finding little evidence of gender bias at the higher levels nowadays.
  • Exhibits of media/culture representation of women and men in mathematics and science:
    • Teen Talk Barbie (1992; withdrawn after protest)
    • Land's End backpacks
    • Allergic to algebra shirt
    • A Beautiful Mind (first 01:15 minute of trailer)
    • Big Bang Theory (see clips at 00:47 and 03:23).
  • Psychology research papers on the effect of attire (dress) on perceived competence of graduate TAs and professors.
